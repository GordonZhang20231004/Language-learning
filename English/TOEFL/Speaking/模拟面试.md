Answer１:
    Yes, I think it's very important to live close to school or work because it can save a lot of time and reduce daily stress.

    For example, when I lived near my university, I could attend early classes more easily and stay at the library late without worrying about transportation.

    I also think living nearby helps people maintain a better work-life balance because they have more time for studying, resting, or social activities.

## Q2：
Interviewer: I see. Imagine that you could choose to commute by car, which is faster but more expensive, or by public transportation, which is slower but less expensive. Which would you choose, and why?

    Answer2:
        If I had to choose, I would probably use public transportation.

        Even though driving is faster, public transportation is much cheaper and less stressful for me. I can use the time to read, listen to music, or just relax a little instead of focusing on traffic all the time.

        I also think public transportation is better for the environment because it reduces the number of cars on the road.

        Of course, it may take more time, but overall, I think the lower cost and more relaxed commuting experience make it a better choice for daily life.

## Q3：
Interviewer: Interesting. Some people believe that commuting can be stressful and tiring. What do you think are one or two different ways to make commuting more enjoyable? Give reasons for your answer.

    Answer3:
        I think there are several ways to make commuting more enjoyable.

        First, listening to podcasts or audiobooks can really help. Instead of feeling bored during the commute, people can learn something new or just relax while listening to interesting content. For example, I often listen to educational podcasts when I’m on the train, and it makes the trip feel shorter and more meaningful.

        Another good way is to avoid crowded commuting hours whenever possible. During rush hour, trains and buses can be stressful and uncomfortable. But if people travel a little earlier or later, they can usually find a seat and enjoy a much calmer ride.

        Overall, I think small changes like these can make commuting much less tiring and more enjoyable.

## Q4：
Interviewer: Good points. Lastly, considering advances in technology, some people believe that commuting might disappear entirely. How do you think a severe decline in commuting might affect businesses in positive ways and negative ways? Please give one example of each.

    Answer4:
        I think a major decline in commuting could have both positive and negative effects on businesses.

        On the positive side, companies could save a lot of money because they would need less office space and fewer utilities. For example, a technology company might reduce office expenses and use that money for new projects or employee benefits.

        However, there could also be negative effects. If employees work remotely all the time, communication and teamwork might become weaker. For instance, a marketing company may find it harder to brainstorm creative ideas online compared to face-to-face discussions.

        So overall, I think reducing commuting could improve efficiency in some ways, but it could also create challenges for teamwork and communication.
