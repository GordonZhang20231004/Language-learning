## Q4:
Interviewer: 
Good points. Let me ask you one final question. For some time now, researchers have been interested in whether green spaces, such as parks, make people who live in cities happier. Do you think that city governments should create more parks in urban areas to promote a general sense of happiness and life satisfaction? Why or why not?

Answer:
    Yes, I believe city governments should prioritize creating more parks. 
    
    Green spaces offer crucial psychological benefits by providing residents with areas to relax and reduce stress, which directly enhances happiness. Moreover, parks encourage physical activity and social interaction, fostering community bonds and improving overall life satisfaction. In dense urban environments, these natural retreats can counterbalance the fast-paced city life, making cities more livable and enjoyable for everyone. Therefore, investing in parks is a wise strategy to promote well-being in urban populations.
